## Profit Margin Analysis: Average Method Impact

### 📊 Your Scenario:

- **Current Stock:** 100 units @ ₹100 each
- **New Purchase:** 50 units @ ₹120 each
- **Profit Margin:** 50%

### 💰 Sale Price Calculations:

#### BEFORE (Using Current Price ₹100):

- **Cost:** ₹100
- **50% Profit:** ₹100 + (50% × ₹100) = ₹100 + ₹50
- **Sale Price:** **₹150**

#### AFTER (Using Average Price ₹106.67):

- **Average Cost:** ₹106.67
- **50% Profit:** ₹106.67 + (50% × ₹106.67) = ₹106.67 + ₹53.33
- **Sale Price:** **₹160**

### 📈 Impact Summary:

- **Sale Price Increase:** ₹160 - ₹150 = **₹10** per unit
- **Percentage Increase:** 6.67%
- **Revenue Impact:** +₹10 per unit sold

### 🎯 Key Insights:

1. **Profit margin stays 50%** but applied to higher average cost
2. **Customers pay ₹10 more** per unit (6.67% increase)
3. **Your margin in rupees increases** from ₹50 to ₹53.33 per unit
4. **Average method automatically adjusts** pricing to maintain profitability

### ⚖️ Business Decision:

- **Average Method:** Simple pricing, all units at ₹160
- **Separate Batches:** Flexibility to price old stock at ₹150, new stock at ₹180

The average method ensures you maintain consistent 50% profit across all inventory, but customers experience a moderate price increase.
